first-semester-thoughts

So I just finished my first semester as a computer science student, my 6th semester in. I think I always kind of knew that this was what I wanted to do. Ever since I was little, I could spend hours infront of a computer screen, not moving, not caring that I had been sedentary for 10 hours. This semester, I ended up taking 6 cs courses:

  • CS 2050: Intro to Discrete Mathematics
  • CS 2110: Computer Organization & Programming
  • CS 2340: Objects and Design
  • CS 3600: Intro to Artificial Intelligence
  • CS 4400: Intro to Database Systems
  • ECE 3811: Vertically Integrated Projects (Intelligent Transportation Systems)

Classes sucked, but thats okay. Besides school, I devoted a lot of time towards finding an internship. To be honest, when I was job seeking, I had low expectations. If I was a recruiter, I’m not even going to lie, I would probably laugh and turn me away. Then shit on me to my coworkers. My only coding experience was changing a little css to make a website responsive. Like. Cmon. I guess somehow though, my little hackathon projects impressed a few people, and I got a couple interviews. It turns out that I’m going to end up working for Turner Broadcasting in the spring as a web dev, and Capital One in the summer as a software engineer. I’m pretty excited about the opportunities, but even more excited about what the future holds!

Apart from cs, I would say that this semester has been pretty depressing in regards to my social life. Frequently, I would wake up, go to class (optional), and then end up coming straight back to my dorm and either working, or sleeping. There wasn’t the same night time thrills of hanging out with friends that I’ve had previous semesters, but I think that’s just a depressing side effect of growing up.
