the philosophy of learning

So this will probably be a multi-post thought. But today when I was doing my semi-regular brainstorming session for personal projects I thought of creating an agent that learns. But this is too easy, I think. Learning is pretty straight-forward. Monkey see, monkey do. Monkey do enough times with sufficient reward, monkey learn. Right?

Instead of that, I want an agent that’s capable of independent thought. This made me question whether new thoughts are even capable outside of the domain of past experiences. For example, when studying gravity, as the story goes, the catalyst for Newton was the apple falling on his head. But what if forces and kinematics were completely unknown to him? How would he process it?
My belief is that all new thoughts are a culmination of past experiences and outside stimuli. If you’re sitting in a white room with no stimuli whatsoever, then all the thoughts in your mind must come from past experiences, right? There’s no possible way that you can think of something that you haven’t experienced. Even if your mind wanders upon something you haven’t seen yet, there must be some memory that made you think of that.

I’m not really sure where I’m going with this but I think it’s an idea worth exploring. If creating a model capable of conscious thought, then perhaps the only way to achieve such a goal is to give it complete domain knowledge; the easiest would namely be the entire internet.
