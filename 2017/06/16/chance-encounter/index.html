chance encounter

On 6/14/17, on my third visit to the local rock climbing gym, I met Frankie.


Frankie. LA. PhD candidate in aerospace engineering. Cornell undergrad. Cornell grad. NASA. Goddard. JPL. The list goes on. I was impressed, but more so because she turned out so socially ept. Frankie, if you ever read this, you’re what I wanted to be when I stepped into Georgia Tech. You resemble the part of me that yearned to do scientific good, to not sell out to computer science. The part of me that kept my wildest dreams alive for 2+ years. I like to think that if I had met current you, during my moment of indecision, maybe you could’ve swayed my mind into staying aerospace. The things you talked about that you do at JPL, man. I don’t know. I barely know you, but I look up to you tremendously. Honestly. You’re an inspiration to my engineering facet.


Ok. Ok. Besides engineering, I’m incredible glad to have met you. This post speaks for itself, I suppose.
I’ve always considered myself a dreamer. Most people don’t see it, hardly anyone actually sees it – I’m self aware. When they look at me, they don’t see me, they don’t see my passion or dreams; they see the persona I’m comfortable with putting on. But throughout the night, I never felt like I had to resort to that. Growing up has made me hard, and I don’t really talk about my dreams, or vision with people I don’t entirely trust. Others, they laugh, they call me stupid for even dreaming. Why? Why? Why live if you’re not going to dream big? What are you even living for? I digress. You didn’t laugh. And you took me seriously – and I am so, so grateful for that.
Out of all the chance encounters I’ve had in my life, this one night was probably the most significant. You pondered what makes a moment significant – I don’t know what to tell you. Perhaps it can’t be quantified; perhaps it’s one of those things that you just know. Hm. Ah. Yes. My initial inspiration for this post was because I was thinking about this conversation and I realized that I want to be what you were to me, Frankie. I want to be significant, if perhaps only fleeting, in someone else’s story. I want to be remembered. To be inspiring, just by being. You didn’t even try – that’s the thing. Just by being you, you inspired me. Thank you.
