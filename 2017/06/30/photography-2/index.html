Today was day 42 in my 100 day challenge. I think initially, I meant to post more updates on my photographic adventures but I’ve just been kinda going along with it – taking photos every 1/2 days. I really have come to appreciate my journey.
I view photography as a means of creative release as well as a window into my life. Photos I take, for the most part, aren’t posed, but rather capture the moments I experience in my life. And I enjoy sharing them! I do. If I had one complaint, though, it would be that I wish I could skip days. Some photos I put on Insta definitely aren’t up to my (newfound) standards. I really try to portray a certain emotion in my photos, but some days, the photos I take don’t have any at all. I don’t like that.
On another note, I kind of impulse purchased a new lens for my camera. I’m SUPER excited to try it out – it’s a Sony 35mm f/1.8 fixed lens, and it should arrive by day 45.

This part isn’t necessarily about photography but I’m going to talk about it anyways because it’s a significant part of recent developments in my life. When I was in Richmond, I took some photos and my friend noted that all the photos I took oozed loneliness. It made me realize that the photos I took, and I guess, take, are a reflection of my current being and feelings. Recently I’ve been taking hella photos of this one person in particular. I guess people can somehow tell, but, yeah, I do really like this girl.
