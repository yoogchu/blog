3 weeks later

Hello hello! It’s been three weeks since school started. I’ve been a little overwhelmed – but just a little.
In order of priorities:

  • CS4476 Computer Vision
  • CS3510 Algorithms
  • CS3630 Robotics and Perception

  • CS3451 Graphics

  • CS4001 Ethics

  • LMC3431 Tech Communications
  • ECE4812 Vertically Integrated Projects (last semester, hallelujah)

    As you can see, there’s a gap between my real classes and my fake classes. My real classes aren’t even that bad, bar algo, but my fake classes will be the death of me, I swear. It’s funny cause comp vis, robotics, and graphics all have overlap so I’ve been learning essentially the same thing three times. I’ve started doing, or trying, to do leetcode each morning for 3 hours but that hasn’t really panned out too well. Dedication is difficult, especially when you wake up but dont even remember turning off your alarm!! Hm. I feel a lot better this semester than previously, though, I think. In terms of work ethic and class attendance, at least.

    What else.. I booked my flight to Grace Hopper yesterday. I’m excited to be able to attend but I don’t really know what to expect. It’s weird – I’ve never felt shy or anxious about being discriminated against, but I’m not going to lie, I’m a little scared to go. I don’t even expect anything out of it; it’s just… hm. You know?

    Careerwise I have absolutely no idea what I’m doing anymore. Constantly conflicted – I don’t want to chase money but I’m not going to act like I don’t consider it a major part of whatever offer I accept. Also interview season is the bane of my existence. I don’t consider what I’ve done impressive – definitely not to the same level as other candidates for the same spot. But I think there’s definitely a great divide in those who are passionate about their work and those who aren’t, and that’s what separates me from other candidates.
    Sure, the easy way out would be to accept my offer and just focus on graduating. But I don’t think I’ve necessarily ever been the type to take the easy way out.
