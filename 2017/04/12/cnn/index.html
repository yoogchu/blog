For the past three months or so, I’ve been working alone on a little file transfer application to improve internal workflow. Today, at the grooming/kickoff meeting, I was actually extremely excited to present my microservice to my coworkers. While going through what it supports and its capabilities, I found myself surprisingly proud of what I had accomplished. Well, honestly, what fills me up with pride, surprisingly, is doing code review. I have this coworker who took on the informal role of being my mentor and every time he reviews my code he’s extremely impressed. To be honest, during code review, I’m extremely impressed by my own code. As Kendrick says, DAMN.

It’s actually funny, when I first began working at CNN, my first two weeks or so, I was completely lost – I had no idea what was going on in the codebase and I was extremely afraid of even writing any code. Honestly, I wanted to quit. But I suppose it’s good that I didn’t because I learned a lot about multithreading, streams, and general devOps type stuff.

If I had one takeaway from this internship that’s winding down, it would be that I’m glad I didn’t give up. Also huge shoutout to my mentor for being outstanding in all capacities, and for putting me on a meaningful project that enabled my growth.
