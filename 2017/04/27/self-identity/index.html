self-identity

I think it’s important that everybody be comfortable with who they are when they’re by themselves. Self-identity is perhaps the most important aspect of who someone really is, yet rarely talked about. This semester, I’ve been spending a lot of time by myself trying to figure out who I am, who my friends are, and how I want to spend my life.


i am … Someone who gets bored easily, and because of this, flirts with danger. Loyal, I think. At least to a point. Someone with a strong aversion towards bullshit and shallowness. Which is hypocritical because this semester I’ve been acting pretty shallow, but I don’t necessarily know if this is a facade I’m putting on because it’s easy or because it’s something I’m slowly transitioning towards. Either way, it kind of makes me sad because the alternative is that I’m unwilling, or rather, cannot, invest myself in someone because I’m scared.

Sounds shitty but friendship is a matter of situation. Fleeting, in a sense. Of course there are exceptions, but I’ve come to realize that everyone is pretty much the same, just footnotes in the book of your life. There was someone I thought worth a couple pages in my college chapter, but I was wrong.

How do I want to spend my life?

We’ll see.
