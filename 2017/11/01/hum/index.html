hum

it’s nights like this where i sorely miss for our summer nights. i want to make my illegal u-turns, pick you up from novelle, and stroll down westpark. i yearn to go our, i guess, now-finished westpark, and sit on the balcony’s edge. i long to hold your hand and squint at the stars, enduring your insistent pointing at stars i struggled to see.

i endure, i suppose, and try to do my work, waiting – waiting, impatiently, for the next thursday i drive up.
