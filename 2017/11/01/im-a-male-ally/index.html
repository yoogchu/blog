im a male ally

I went to Grace Hopper as a male, yes. And it was more impactful than I thought it would be.


I guess I’ll start with the obvious question: why would a male want to go to Grace Hopper? Why do you, a male, want to go to a conference that celebrates Women in Technology?

I don’t think equality in the industry is just a woman’s problem. There was a tech talk that especially stood out to me, and I tell everyone who asks about my experience at Grace Hopper about it.

“A woman is a doctor, a man is a nurse.” This phrase is put into Google Translate, translated to a gender-neutral language like Turkish or something, then translated back. “A man is a doctor, a woman is a nurse.”

This utterly blew my mind. Inherent bias is going to exist whether we like it or not. And having men dominate the technology field in terms of representation will lead to biased technology. People like to think that technology is fair and that bias doesn’t exist. This may have been true in the past with deterministic programs, but when new technologies are being trained with questionable data, this is no longer the case. Diversity is important in the industry.


I guess the part that impacted me most as a male is the technological impact, but my intent isn’t to diminish the importance of cultural equality either. I may not be a woman, but I know what it feels like to be judged by my characteristics, to be put down just because I belong to a certain group, and it’s a shitty feeling. Nobody deserves to feel like that – and it shouldn’t hold anyone back. But it does, and it needs to change. It’s not just a woman’s fight. As long as only women fight for equality in the workplace, it’s just a woman’s issue. But it’s not, and everyone should be aware and do what they can.
